The Adventure of Papua

The Bag of Culture: Noken

The Noken is a traditional Papuan bag, handwoven from tree fibers or orchid stems. This versatile and eco-friendly bag is not just a carrying tool but also a symbol of Papuan cultural identity, recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage. Each pattern and weaving technique represents different tribal traditions and stories.

The White Gold of Papua: Papuan White Shell

The white shell, known locally as 'Kina', holds immense cultural and economic value in Papua. These shells were traditionally used as currency and ceremonial objects. Today, they remain important in cultural ceremonies and as symbols of wealth and status in Papuan society.

The Wonderful of Papua: Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat, meaning 'Four Kings', is a stunning archipelago comprising over 1,500 small islands. Known for having the richest marine biodiversity on Earth, this paradise showcases Papua's natural wonder with its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and unique island formations.

The Hope From Papua: Papuan Dream Catcher

A typical Papuan dreamcatcher is made from rattan or bamboo with a woven sago or pandan fiber net. Decorated with Cenderawasih bird feathers, shell beads, and Asmat or Dani tribal motif carvings, this dreamcatcher symbolizes protection, good luck, and a spiritual connection with nature and ancestors. In addition to functioning as a filter for bad dreams, its beauty also makes it a valuable work of art.
