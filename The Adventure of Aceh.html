The Adventure of Aceh

Sie Reuboh

Sie Reuboh is a mandatory dish during the meugang tradition. One family will cook sie reuboh together and eat it together. Because it lasts a long time, sie reuboh can also be stored as a sahur dish in the following days during the Ramadan fast.

The Sumatran rhinoceros (Dicerorhinus sumatrensis) is the smallest and most primitive of all rhino species, distinguished by its body hair. The ajak (Cuon alpinus), a wild forest dog native to South, East, and Southeast Asia, including Sumatra and Java, inhabits mountainous and forested areas and is distinct from wolves. The Sumatran elephant (Elephas maximus sumatranus), a critically endangered subspecies of the Asian elephant, is native to Sumatra and faces the threat of extinction.

The Extinct Animal

The Sumatran tiger is a population of Panthera tigris sondaica that inhabits the island of Sumatra, Indonesia, and is the only surviving member of the Sunda tiger subspecies. It is classified as critically endangered on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species. The wild population is estimated at between 400-500 individuals.
