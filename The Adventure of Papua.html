Patung

This sculpture depicts a Papuan man sitting cross-legged in a meditative pose. With a jet-black body, he has white and red streaks adorning his body. The dominant red color symbolizes courage, spirit, and strength. The red headdress resembles feathers, adding to his majestic appearance. This sculpture is not only a beautiful display, but also a representation of Papua's rich and meaningful culture.

Paper Mache Mask

These masks are from the island of Papua Indonesia, these types of mask's have a very important role In their culture. They can be used in rituals, and in traditional ceremonys, in their rituals and ceremonys these masks are a form of communication with the gods. Alot of the mask's are also replicas of the gods faces so that the hod is truly never forgotten

The Hope From Papua: Papuan Dream Catcher

The Hope From Papua is a dream catcher inspired by the natural beauty of Papua, which is often referred to as the hidden pearl of Indonesia. With a combination of natural materials such as feathers, beads, and threads, this dream catcher symbolizes purity, beauty, and peace. Every detail reflects the richness of Papua's culture and natural charm, making it more than just a decoration, but also a symbol of serenity and good luck.
