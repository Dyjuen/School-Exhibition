The Adventure of Sulawesi

Replica of "Pisang Ijo"

Pisang ijo ice come from Makassar (South Sulawesi). The ingredients used is bananas, marrow porridge, green dough, syrup and ice. it symbolize a harmonious balance of life. The green color used symbolizes sacredness and holiness. This food has a sweet taste so it is often used as a dessert or served in hot weather.

Replica of "Panada"

Panada comes from Manado which is in north sulawesi. With the influence of Portuguese culture, panada is bread/cake with minced fish/meat filling. usually The fish is kipjack fish and seasoned with spices. Panada is often used as a snack food that has been passed down from generation to generation by Sulawesi residents.

Miniature of Tambi traditional house

Tambi traditional house is a traditional house from the Tidung island, South Sulawesi. The house is made from wood and bamboo. The house is a traditional house that has been passed down from generation to generation by the residents of Tidung island. The Tambi Traditional House has the meaning of fertility and prosperity for its residents. The decoration of buffalo or cow horns at the end of the roof, called pebaula, symbolizes human sacrifice to the Creator. This house is in the form of a stilt house, because it is to avoid attacks by wild animals.

Painting of a Phinisi Ship

The Pinisi ship is originated from South Sulawesi, and was used by the Bugis people in navigating the seas of the archipelago. These ships were often used as cargo ships in the past for trade. According to THE legend, the Pinisi Ship was first built by Sawerigading, the crown prince of the Luwu Kingdom, in the 14th century. The ship was rebuilt by the local community after breaking into three pieces due to a major storm, and has since been known as the Pinisi Ship.

Badik

Badik is a traditional weapon originating from South Sulawesi, specificly developed by the Bugis, Makassar, and Mandar tribes. This weapon has become an integral part of the culture and identity of the people. At first, badik was used as a tool for daily activities such as farming and hunting. In addition, badik functions as a self-defense tool from threats. Over time, the badik developed into a symbol of self-esteem and courage, known as the concept of "siri'" in Bugis-Makassar culture.
